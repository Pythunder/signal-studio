Introduction to Signal Studio 

Signal Studio is a free, Open-Source Signal Analyzer and Editor  for Windows XP / Vista / 7 / 8 / 8.1 / 10 . Unlike other apps, Signal Studio is perfect , meaning samples aren't altered unless they need to be. Editing a portion of a signal/sound file only affects that portion; the rest of the file is untouched. Blocks of audio can be cut and pasted without changing their contents at all. This is especially useful for patching a finished master without corrupting its dither. Make sure to run the program as Administrator on systems that doesn't save the program's settings on relaunch.

To get started, Open an Audio File (MP3/Wav/or any supported file) and perform various actions. You can also select a section to apply the effects or to perform actions only to that selection. This applies to cut/copy too. You can also perform additional effects/operations from the Plugins menu.

To Generate a new Signal, use the Generate... option from the Audio Menu from the Menu Bar.

 

With Signal Studio, you can:

  • Play almost all audio files.
  • Edit files with absolute precision (can edit 1/1000000th of a second) or (0.000001 part of a second)!
  • Convert audio files from one format to the other.
  • Apply special effects, LADSPA plugins.
  • Enhance quality.
  • Perform corrections.
  • Edit/Modify/Remove any section from an audio file.
  • Analyze, research and learn sounds and their waveform.
  • Cut audio.
  • Amplify weak audios.
  • Merge two or more files into a single file.
  • Record your voice straight from your mic.
  • Seperate the music part alone from song [Karaoke].
  • Mix, match and create your own music tracks.
  • Modify the metadata of a file.
  • Reduce the size of an audio file.
  • Apply reverse, fade and thousands of other high quality effects.
  • Perfect Sound Editing makes only changes to only the required portion of an audio, thereby maintaining the dither.
  • Performs bit-wise editing with absolute quality and with zero distorsion.
  • Modulate the audio signal from one form to the other.
  • Analyze and experiment with signals
  • Generate Signals of different frequencies with different modulations.
  • Apply modulation,shifting,distortion,cleanup,filters,effects to signals.
  • and much more!
